1. Introduction

GoTrust WDU (TUR) is a fixed-supply BEP-20 utility token deployed on the BNB Smart Chain. The project focuses on transparency, educational use cases, and verifiable on-chain behavior.

This whitepaper provides a complete, non-promotional description of the token's design, implementation, and non-financial nature.

2. Project Purpose

GoTrust WDU serves as an educational demonstration of a simple, transparent BEP-20 token implementation.

The project does not involve fundraising, investment promotion, profit expectations, or financial claims of any kind.

3. What This Project Is NOT

  • No ICO, IEO, IDO, or token sale
  • No investment product or security
  • No profit, yield, or return expectations
  • No DAO, foundation, or company structure
  • No minting, burning, or administrative controls

4. Smart Contract Design

The smart contract is a standard BEP-20 implementation with the following fixed properties:

  • No mint function
  • No burn function
  • 0% transaction taxes
  • No blacklist or pause features
  • No ownership override

The contract is fully verified and immutable on BscScan.

5. Token Distribution

100% of the total supply was created at deployment and allocated to the deployer wallet.

No pre-mined tokens, team reserves, or allocation schedules exist.

11. Token Holding Transparency

100% of tokens are held in the deployer wallet at deployment.

No distribution or transfer occurred during or after deployment.

The presence of tokens in the deployer wallet does not represent:

  • An investment reserve
  • A treasury
  • A fundraising pool
  • A promise of future distribution
  • A financial control mechanism

The deployer wallet functions as a transparent on-chain holding address, fully visible and verifiable through BNB Smart Chain explorers.

All token balances and movements (if any) remain publicly auditable and comply with BscScan.

20. Non-Custodial & No Control Statement

The GoTrust WDU smart contract does not grant the deployer or any address special privileges, administrative rights, or control mechanisms.

Holding tokens in a wallet does not provide the ability to:

  • Modify the contract
  • Mint additional tokens
  • Restrict transfers
  • Alter balances programmatically

Token ownership is governed solely by the immutable smart contract logic and standard BEP-20 transfer rules.
